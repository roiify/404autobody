What our collision repair covers

A modern car is a safety system, not just sheet metal. A crash can compromise the crumple zones, the unibody, and the sensors that run automatic braking and lane-keep. Repairing the visible dent without restoring that structure leaves the car looking fine but unsafe in the next impact.

We repair the whole picture:

  • Unibody & frame straightening to factory datum points
  • Structural welding & bonding on steel and aluminum bodies
  • Panel replacement, alignment and precise panel gaps
  • Bumper, fender, quarter-panel and door repair or replacement
  • Factory-matched paint and clearcoat blend
  • Suspension & wheel-alignment checks after structural work

Why "straight" isn't enough

Insurance-driven "quick" repairs often skip the measuring bench. We start every structural job on a computerized measuring system that compares your car against the manufacturer's original dimensions in three axes — so the frame is provably back to spec, within roughly 1mm of factory datum, before any panel goes on.

Our repair process

1 · Damage assessment

A full teardown and computerized measure documents every millimeter of deviation — hidden structural damage included — before a tool touches the car. You get a written estimate and timeline.

2 · Structural repair

Frame and unibody pulled back to factory spec on the bench, with certified welds and OEM parts where the manufacturer requires them.

3 · Panel & refinish

Panels hung and gapped, then color-matched with a spectrophotometer and sprayed in a downdraft booth for a factory-grade finish.

4 · Glass & ADAS recalibration

New glass and seals as needed, then every driver-assist camera and radar sensor recalibrated to spec so automatic braking and lane-keep work as designed.

5 · Cut, polish & QC

Final detail, a road test, and a quality-control inspection. We don't hand back a car we wouldn't drive ourselves.

6 · Insurance handled

We work with all major carriers and manage the claim paperwork and supplements directly, so you have one point of contact from drop-off to pickup.

Collision repair questions

Do I have to use the shop my insurer recommends?

No. New York law gives you the right to choose your own repair shop — your insurer cannot require you to use theirs. We'll work directly with any carrier.

Will my car be as safe as before the crash?

That's the goal of a structural repair: restore the frame to factory dimensions and recalibrate the safety sensors so crumple zones and driver-assist systems perform as designed.

How long does collision repair take?

Most repairs run 3–7 business days depending on parts and severity. You get a written timeline at drop-off and status updates along the way.

Do you use OEM parts?

We follow OEM repair procedures and use manufacturer parts where they're required for structural or safety integrity; quality alternatives are only used with your approval.
